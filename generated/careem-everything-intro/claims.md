- **Generic framing:** “Your day has places, cravings, errands, and people to pay.” This is non-factual scene-setting, not a market claim.

- **Generic framing:** “Morning commute or quick hop. Pick your way across town.” This describes common transport use without making a performance or availability claim.

- **Opinion:** “the tasty corner of Careem” is playful creative language, not a quality claim.
- **Paraphrase:** Get includes Quik/groceries, Quik Electronics, Supermarkets, Box, Home Services, Flowers, and Pharmacy. Source: homepage Get category tile.
- **Inference, visibly framed as metaphor:** “Get turns that untidy list into one neat shortcut.” This summarizes the grouped Get services and does not claim a measured outcome.
- **Paraphrase:** Pay includes Pay, Donations, and Send money. Source: homepage Pay category tile.
- **Verbatim:** “Go anywhere. Eat anytime. Get anything. Pay anyone.” Source: homepage category headings and subheadings.
- **Creative close:** “Different errands, one bright green wink, one app ready for your next move.” This is brand-led creative language; it makes no numeric, comparative, or market claim.
