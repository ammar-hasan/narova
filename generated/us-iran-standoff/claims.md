# Claims ledger — US–Iran standoff briefing (as of ~21 July 2026)

Every factual assertion spoken in `vo`, tagged verbatim / paraphrase / inference,
against a source. Deliberately balanced across Western, Iranian, and Chinese-facing
reporting, with concrete figures on the Eastern (energy / China) side.

## Scene 2 — origins (how we got here)
- **"Feb 28, 2026 — Israel and the U.S. attacked Iran and killed its supreme leader, Ali Khamenei"** — paraphrase of reported fact.
  On 28 Feb 2026 Israel and the US launched a war against Iran and assassinated Iranian supreme
  leader Ali Khamenei. Sources: Wikipedia "2026 Lebanon war" / "Timeline of the 2026 Lebanon war";
  The National (2026-07-11, "Mojtaba Khamenei vows revenge for father's killing").
- **"spilled into Lebanon — 2,000+ killed, ~1M displaced, before a shaky truce"** — verbatim figures.
  By mid-April 2026 more than 2,000 killed in Lebanon and over one million displaced; Israel–
  Hezbollah renewed a truce mid-June 2026. Sources: CNN (2026-06-19/06-20), Wikipedia "2026 Lebanon war".
- **"the July strikes came mid-conflict"** — inference from the above timeline (framing, not a new stat).

## Scene 2 — the deal
- **"June 17 — Islamabad memorandum, 14 points, 60-day clock"** — paraphrase.
  US and Iran signed a 14-point MoU ("Islamabad Memorandum") on 17 June 2026, opening a
  60-day window to reach a final deal. Sources: CNN (2026-07-01), Al Jazeera (2026-07-01),
  Wikipedia "2025–2026 Iran–United States negotiations".
- **"reopen Hormuz, freeze Lebanon, ease sanctions; Para 9 = no new sanctions, no new forces"** — paraphrase + verbatim.
  MoU covered reopening the Strait of Hormuz, a ceasefire in Lebanon protecting Hezbollah (so long
  as it doesn't fire at Israel), easing financial restrictions, and later nuclear talks. Paragraph 9:
  Iran maintains the nuclear status quo; the US "will not impose any new sanctions and will not
  deploy additional forces in the region." US issued a 60-day oil-sanctions waiver (2026-06-22).
  Sources: CNN live (2026-07-10), Al Jazeera (2026-06-22), CNN (2026-06-20, Lebanon), NPR MoU text.

## Scene 4 — who broke it (both sides)
- **"Iran struck commercial tankers in the strait; threatened to close it"** — paraphrase.
  Iran struck multiple commercial ships in the Strait of Hormuz on 6–7 July 2026 (Al Rekayyat,
  Wedyan, Kiku) and repeatedly threatened/closed the strait. Source: Wikipedia "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis".
- **"Washington revoked the oil waiver, imposed fresh sanctions, kept hitting Iranian soil — all forbidden by Para 9"** — paraphrase + sourced.
  OFAC revoked General License X (the oil-sanctions waiver) effective 7 July 2026 (GL X1 = wind-down
  only); the US imposed fresh sanctions on 10 and 14 July; and conducted airstrikes on Iranian
  territory — directly against Para 9's "no new sanctions / no new forces." Iran's spokesman Baghaei
  said the US "blatantly breached" the MoU via military attacks, revoking the waiver, and new sanctions.
  Sources: Steptoe/OFAC updates (2026-06/07), US State Dept sanctions actions (07-10, 07-14),
  Iran International (2026-07-11), Middle East Eye, CNN (2026-07-10).
- **"Araghchi: 'there can only be mutual compliance'"** — verbatim quote.
  Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi accused the US of violating the MoU after fresh sanctions: "Iran has so
  far kept its word, unlike the … U.S. Treasury Secretary who is violating Para 9 of the MoU… There
  can only be mutual compliance." Sources: Benzinga (2026-07), Islam Times, Middle East Eye.

## Scene 5 — the war of words
- **"Trump declared the deal 'over'; called Iran's leaders 'scum,' 'liars,' 'sick people'; threatened a nuclear site"** — verbatim quotes.
  Trump declared the MoU "over" (8 July); called Iran's leaders "scum," "liars," "sick people," said
  he no longer wanted to negotiate; and threatened Iran's "Pickaxe Mountain" nuclear site as a
  "possible target for a nice big fat shot." Sources: Press TV (2026-07-14), Wikipedia "2026 Iran war", Fox News.
- **"Pezeshkian: insults are 'worthy of them, not the Iranian nation'; Iran is in a 'full-scale war'"** — verbatim quotes.
  Pezeshkian dismissed Trump's "disrespectful remarks" as "worthy of them, not the Iranian nation,"
  and said "the Islamic Republic of Iran is engaged in a full-scale war." Sources: Press TV (2026-07-14),
  Gulf News, Wikipedia.

## Scene 4 — why Hormuz matters
- **"~20 million barrels a day — about a fifth of the world's oil"** — verbatim figure.
  ~20 mb/d of crude + products transited Hormuz in 2025; ~20% of global oil / ~25% of seaborne
  oil trade. Sources: IEA Strait of Hormuz Factsheet (Feb 2026), US EIA, Britannica.
- **"fell almost 30%, to 14.6 million; clawed back to ~8.5 million"** — verbatim figures.
  Q1 2026 flows fell ~30% YoY to 14.6 mb/d; mid-July ~8.5M barrels transited on a Sunday
  (partial recovery). Sources: Bloomberg / EIA (2026-05-13), CNBC (2026-07-13).

## Scene 5 — the oil shock
- **"July 14 — reinstated blockade + 20% fee on every cargo"** — paraphrase.
  Trump announced Hormuz blockade + 20% fee "on all cargo shipped", effective 14 July.
  Source: Yahoo Finance (2026-07).
- **"Brent +9.5%, past $83; touched $126 in March"** — verbatim figures.
  Brent futures jumped 9.5% above $83/barrel (WTI >$78); Brent had peaked at $126 in March 2026.
  Sources: Yahoo Finance (2026-07), Wikipedia "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis".
- **"Iran: passage is 'currently unfeasible'"** — verbatim quote.
  Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority: "passage through the Strait of Hormuz is currently
  unfeasible." Source: Yahoo Finance / wire (2026-07).

## Scene 6 — China's exposure
- **"80–90% of Iran's oil exports go to China; ~1.4M b/d"** — verbatim figures.
  China imported ~1.4 mb/d from Iran (end 2025) = ~13% of China's crude imports and
  80–90% of Iran's total oil exports. Sources: Bruegel, chinadata.live, tradingeconomics.
- **"China's crude imports crashed from 11.6M to 7.8M b/d — an 8-year low"** — verbatim figures.
  China's crude imports fell to ~7.8 mb/d (May 2026), down ~29–33% from ~11.6 mb/d pre-war;
  an eight-year low. Sources: Energy Connects (2026-06), Bruegel.

## Scene 7 — the $400B bet
- **"25-year deal, 2021 — $400B pledged; ~$27B actual over 15 years"** — paraphrase + verbatim.
  Iran–China 25-Year Cooperation Program signed 27 March 2021; ~$400B Chinese investment
  pledged for discounted oil. Critics note cumulative Chinese investment over ~15 years was
  only ~$27B. Sources: Wikipedia "Iran–China 25-year Cooperation Program", Times of Israel,
  International Crisis Group.

## Scene 8 — Beijing's play
- **"Wang Yi and Ishaq Dar jointly urged both sides back to talks; China wants Iran to 'follow through'"** — paraphrase + verbatim.
  China FM Wang Yi and Pakistan's Ishaq Dar jointly called for the US and Iran to stop fighting
  and resume talks (Shanghai, ~17 July 2026); China pressed Iran to "follow through" on the deal.
  Sources: China Global South Project (2026-07-17), Breitbart (2026-07-09).
- **"Iran calls China's role 'constructive and positive'"** — verbatim quote.
  Iran's FM spokesman Baghaei said China played a "constructive and positive role" in
  ceasefire / negotiation efforts. Source: Xinhua via wire.

## Scene 9 — the nuclear shadow
- **"IAEA locked out since Feb 28; no inspectors, no cameras"** — paraphrase.
  IAEA stopped verification activities in Iran after 28 Feb 2026; near-total loss of monitoring.
  Sources: ISIS report (June 2026), IAEA GOV/2026/8, Euronews (2026-06-24).
- **"more than 400 kg of 60% uranium unaccounted for; weapons-grade is ~90%"** — paraphrase + figure.
  IAEA calculated 440.9 kg of uranium enriched up to 60% U-235 on the eve of the June 2025
  attacks; now unaccounted for. Weapons-grade HEU is conventionally ~90% U-235. Sources: ISIS
  report (June 2026), Arms Control Association.
- **"enough for up to 10 weapons, if Iran chose to"** — paraphrase.
  Tehran believed to hold enough HEU to potentially construct up to 10 nuclear weapons if it
  chose to. Sources: Euronews (2026-06-24), Arms Control Association.

## Scene 10 — close
- **"a deal that's not dead — both capitals still say they'll talk"** — paraphrase.
  Both sides say they remain committed to negotiations despite the truce collapse; efforts
  described as fragile. Sources: Al Jazeera, CNN (2026-07-10).

## Framing note
Each government's position is reported as a claim attributed to that government, not as
established fact — balancing US, Iranian, and Chinese framings. Infographic bar widths are
scaled proportionally to the cited figures (e.g. Hormuz 20/14.6/8.5 mb/d; China 11.6/7.8 mb/d;
deal $400B vs $27B; enrichment 60% against a ~90% weapons-grade ceiling).
